Comment-Driven Research Roadmap

Operational Plan for Turning the Sequential Homeostatic RL Project into a Publishable Paper

This document converts advisor comments from the annotated PDF into a concrete, executable, and falsifiable research program. The goal is not just to "run more experiments." The goal is to sharpen the paper around a mechanism-level claim that can survive reviewer questions.

Target outcome: submission-quality empirical paper
Core pivot: boundary-aware homeostatic regulation
Status: operational design drafted on 2026-03-30
Recommended publishable claim: Homeostatic reward is most useful not as a full replacement for task reward inside each task, but as a task-general constraint signal that preserves safe, reusable state across task boundaries. The cleanest paper tests exactly when that claim is true, when it is false, and why.
Why This Pivot

What the Comments Are Really Asking Us to Do

Comment theme 1: narrow the literature and the contribution. "This is a vast literature..." means the paper should stop trying to cover everything about grounding, survival, and general transfer, and instead anchor itself tightly in sequential transfer under embodied constraints.
Comment theme 2: disambiguate reward design. "Homeostatic on its own, or in addition to task-specific rewards?" means the paper needs an explicit reward-factor experiment, not an implicit assumption.
Comment theme 3: make task boundaries causal. Multiple comments about energy state across tasks imply that the real mechanism may live at the boundary between tasks, not only inside tasks. This should become a first-class experimental variable.
Comment theme 4: speak the language of continual RL. The note about "backwards transfer" means we should align the framing, metrics, and plots with the continual RL literature so the contribution reads as recognizable and publishable.
Paper Positioning

Minimal Publishable Scope

Keep

A sequential gridworld benchmark with shared energy dynamics, fixed task order, and multiple seeds.

Transfer, forgetting, safety margin, and energy-state carryover as the core metrics.

De-emphasize

Broad "symbol grounding" language unless you can back it with separate representation analyses.

Any claim that homeostatic reward fully replaces task reward in general.

Center

The mechanism that a task-general homeostatic signal stabilizes cross-task state and preserves later solvability.

The interaction between reward type and boundary condition.

Core Variables

The Factorial Design the Paper Should Revolve Around

Axis Condition Why it matters Interpretation
Reward regime Task-only Baseline that optimizes each task locally. If this wins everywhere, the homeostatic story is weak.
Reward regime Task + Homeostatic Main candidate for publishable contribution. If this wins specifically when boundary state matters, the paper has a sharp mechanism.
Reward regime Pure Homeostatic Important ablation for the advisor's question. If it underperforms within tasks but helps with carryover, that supports the refined claim.
Boundary condition Reset All tasks begin from the same initial energy. If the advantage disappears here, the mechanism is likely boundary-specific.
Boundary condition Carryover Task t begins from the terminal energy state of task t-1. This is the key causal manipulation suggested by the comments.
Difficulty regime Loose, Tight Tests whether safety margin matters only when resources are truly scarce. The claim is stronger if the effect scales with scarcity.
Hypotheses

Operational Hypotheses and What We Do If They Fail

H1

Boundary-specific utility hypothesis

Primary

The Task + Homeostatic agent will outperform Task-only on forward transfer, survival, and final solvability when energy is carried across tasks, but not necessarily when energy is reset between tasks.

If supported
Write the paper around a mechanistic interaction: homeostatic reward is especially useful as a boundary stabilizer under shared embodied constraints.
If not supported
Either the carryover manipulation is too weak, or the main claim should shift toward "homeostatic reward does not add value beyond task reward in this benchmark."
H2

Pure homeostatic reward is not the best general solution

Clarifying

Pure Homeostatic will likely learn simple within-task objectives more slowly than Task-only or Task + Homeostatic, even if it produces higher end-of-task energy.

If supported
This strengthens the refined narrative: homeostatic reward should be auxiliary or cross-task, not a universal replacement for task reward.
If not supported
If pure homeostatic dominates widely, the paper can claim a stronger result, but then it needs tighter controls to show the effect is not just a better shaping signal.
H3

Safety margin matters more in tight regimes

Robustness

The benefit of homeostatic regularization should be larger when tasks are tight enough that arriving at the end of one task in a depleted state makes the next task materially harder.

If supported
Add a figure showing the interaction between reward regime and scarcity. This becomes one of the strongest publishable results.
If not supported
The benchmark may not be resource-sensitive enough, or the effect may be smaller than initially thought. Tighten margins or reduce the claim.
Experiment Ladder

Executable Study Plan

E0

Instrumentation and sanity checks

Must do first

Before any claim, make sure the benchmark can actually express the mechanism we care about.

Required implementation

  • Add a config flag for boundary_mode = reset | carryover.
  • Log terminal energy at the end of each task phase.
  • Log initial energy at the start of each next task.
  • Record per-task success, deaths, hazard hits, and remaining energy.

Verification

  • Run 3 seeds and inspect raw logs.
  • Confirm that reset mode truly overwrites the state.
  • Confirm that carryover mode truly propagates the previous terminal energy.

Failure signal

  • If distributions of initial energy are almost identical between modes, the manipulation is not active enough.
E1

Reproduce the current pilot under reset and carryover

Establish baseline

Compare the current sequential setup under two boundary modes while keeping the existing agents. This tells us whether the boundary manipulation itself changes the story.

Conditions

  • Task-only + reset
  • Task-only + carryover
  • Pure Homeostatic + reset
  • Pure Homeostatic + carryover

Metrics

  • Phase-end success matrix
  • Forgetting per task
  • Forward transfer against single-task baselines
  • Remaining energy and death rate

Decision rule

  • If carryover barely changes outcomes, the paper cannot center task-boundary energy.
  • If carryover hurts task-only much more than homeostatic, move immediately to E2.
E2

Main factorial study: reward regime x boundary mode

Main figure candidate

This is the central experiment for the paper. If this experiment is clean, the paper is viable.

Reward regime Reset Carryover
Task-only baseline local optimization tests collapse under poor boundary state
Task + Homeostatic tests auxiliary effect without boundary pressure main publishable condition
Pure Homeostatic tests replacement strategy tests whether pure homeostasis survives transitions

Recommended seeds

  • Pilot: 5 seeds per cell
  • Main run: 20 seeds per cell
  • Use matched seed sets across cells

Minimum evidence threshold

  • Task + Homeostatic beats Task-only in carryover on at least 2 of: forward transfer, death rate, final success, boundary-start solvability.
  • The same advantage should shrink or disappear in reset mode.

Statistical plan

  • Bootstrap 95% CI for means
  • Mann-Whitney or permutation test for pairwise comparisons
  • Report effect sizes, not only p-values
E3

Tightness sweep and failure-boundary stress test

Mechanism confirmation

Sweep task tightness so that cross-task safety margin becomes weak, medium, or strong. This tests whether the homeostatic effect scales with actual embodied pressure.

Sweep dimensions

  • Initial energy
  • Step cost
  • Food gain
  • Hazard cost

Main readout

  • Does the mixed agent's advantage grow as the environment becomes tighter?
  • Does the task-only agent increasingly finish tasks in unrecoverable states?

Interpretation

  • If yes, the mechanism is plausibly real.
  • If no, the paper should avoid a strong safety-margin mechanism claim.
E4

Observation vs reward disambiguation

Reviewer defense

Add a control that sees energy but is not rewarded for homeostatic regulation. This distinguishes the value of observing internal state from the value of optimizing it.

Needed conditions

  • Task-only + Energy Observation
  • Task + Homeostatic + Energy Observation

Question answered

  • Is the benefit because the agent knows its state?
  • Or because the optimization objective changes?

Priority

  • Do this only after E2 works, unless a reviewer-like concern appears early.
E5

Single-task oracle comparison

Scope boundary

Train task-specific oracle agents independently on each task. The goal is not to beat them. The goal is to show the cost of specialization and the value of reusability.

If oracle wins within task
That is expected. The paper can still win by showing better cross-task efficiency, lower redesign cost, or stronger boundary robustness.
If sequential agent also loses transfer
The benchmark may not support the central claim. Reduce the claim or redesign the tasks before writing the paper.
Metrics

What Must Be Logged for the Results to Be Verifiable

Per-episode logs

  • task id, seed, phase index
  • start energy and end energy
  • success or failure
  • death cause if failed
  • hazard hits
  • food collected or recharge events
  • episode length
  • reward decomposition if using mixed reward

Derived metrics

  • current-task success curve
  • phase-end success matrix r_i,j
  • forgetting per task
  • forward transfer against single-task baselines
  • backward transfer
  • mean remaining energy on successful episodes
  • boundary solvability: probability that task t+1 is solvable from terminal state of task t

Execution Order

Concrete Week-by-Week Plan

Week 1
Implement boundary_mode, per-task boundary logging, and mixed reward decomposition. Finish E0 and a 3-seed sanity pass for E1. Do not move on until raw logs confirm the manipulation.
Week 2
Run E1 and small E2 pilot with 5 seeds per cell. Decide whether carryover is strong enough to justify the paper. If not, adjust environment tightness before scaling.
Week 3
Lock the benchmark and run the main E2 experiment at 20 seeds per cell. Produce preliminary plots and identify whether the main interaction exists.
Week 4
Run E3 tightness sweep and one disambiguation study from E4. Start drafting figures and the methods section in parallel.
Week 5
Add oracle comparison if needed, finalize statistics, and write the paper around the strongest supported claim. Only keep experiments that sharpen the final narrative.
Decision Tree

How the Story Changes Depending on the Results

Best-case outcome

Task + Homeostatic wins in carryover mode, the advantage shrinks in reset mode, and the effect grows under tighter resource margins.

Paper title direction: homeostatic reward as a reusable cross-task stabilizer under embodied constraints.

Mixed outcome

Pure homeostatic helps safety but hurts within-task performance; mixed reward is only modestly better.

Paper title direction: when and where homeostatic reward helps sequential RL, with a bounded empirical claim.

Negative outcome

Boundary carryover does not change much, or task-only remains best everywhere.

Fallback paper: negative evidence that homeostatic reward does not improve transfer in this benchmark, plus analysis of why the mechanism failed to emerge.

Still publishable if careful

Even a negative result can be useful if the benchmark is clean, the controls are clear, and the failure mode is informative about when homeostatic objectives are not enough.

Writing Checklist

What the Final Paper Must Explicitly Say

A
Why boundary carryover is the causal manipulation, not a side detail.
B
Why task reward and homeostatic reward should be compared both separately and jointly.
C
Why the benchmark is a transfer benchmark under shared embodied constraints, not a claim about all continual RL.
D
Why safety margin is measured behaviorally and mechanistically, not only through return.
E
What the strongest supported claim is, and which stronger claims are intentionally not made.

A publishable paper is not the one with the most experiments. It is the one where the causal story, the controls, and the final claim line up cleanly. The comments suggest that the cleanest story here is not "homeostatic reward solves everything," but "homeostatic reward matters most when reusable internal-state regulation is needed across task boundaries."
